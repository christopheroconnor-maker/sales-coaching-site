Coaching Points

1
Qualify before the calendar invite — >€700k online revenue only

Before booking any Plus meeting ask: does the merchant have >€700k in online revenue? If not, the meeting should not happen. This single change cuts ~30% of meeting volume and increases win rate significantly — the same hours on far fewer, far more closeable deals.

2
One anchor number per call — pick it before you join

Identify the single most impactful saving for that merchant (usually their monthly transaction fee cost at current volume). Open with it. Build the whole call around it. Every other benefit — partners, B2B, checkout features — is supporting evidence, not a co-headline.

3
Replace "I'll send you X" with a two-option close, in the meeting

At the 20-minute mark: "Does it make more sense to start with the 60-day trial so you can see the results yourself, or would you rather move to the full plan with the £2,500 credit applied to year one?" Force the decision now, not next week.

2
Ask "what's your #1 challenge this year?" — listen before presenting

Anchor value before naming price — every time

Build to the annual saving figure first. Then reveal Plus costs €2,000/month. Then show the net. Never say €2,000 first. And when the merchant reacts positively to the saving — stop presenting and ask: "Does it make sense to start the trial today?"

1
30-minute hard cap — set a timer on every call

At 25 minutes, if no decision is on the table: "I want to respect your time. Based on [the saving number], does the trial make sense to start this week, or do you want to go straight to the full plan?" Then stop. His 50-minute calls are not converting more — they're introducing doubt.

2
Close the moment the cost analysis lands — not after it

When the merchant says "that's impressive" or reacts positively to the saving number — that is the close window. Not a partner slide later. Not a feature mention later. Right there: "So based on that, does it make sense to get the trial started today?"

3
One incentive per call, named upfront — not stacked when hesitation appears

Pick the most relevant one for that merchant and introduce it as part of the trial offer. "The trial comes with a free CRO audit from Quickfire — that's the one I'd focus on for your business" is far stronger than listing four sweeteners when the merchant pauses.

Cite a specific £/€/$ number in every single call — make it a rule

28% ROI citation rate is the lowest of the six. Before every meeting, identify the one guaranteed saving (transaction fees at their GMV) and commit to naming it within the first 10 minutes. Separate the certain ROI from projected uplifts — merchants who want guarantees need to hear the certain number first.
