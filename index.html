What Makes Sean Work

  • ROI-first: nearly every call opens with a specific fee saving calculation (£7k annual savings, $8.6k–$17k monthly uplift, 0.6% third-party removal)

How He Differs from Seán Quinn

  • Needs 40% more calls (322 vs 230) to achieve similar revenue — efficiency gap
  • Win rate 8pts lower despite more volume — some pipeline lacks qualification rigour
  • Commitment resistance (contract term objections) sometimes stalls deals that should close

What the Team Should Steal from Sean

1
Build the fee analysis before the call — then open with the number

Sean regularly walks into meetings with "$8,600–$17,000 monthly uplift" or "£7,000 annual saving" already calculated for that specific merchant. This anchors the conversation immediately. Any rep who shows up without the merchant's specific saving pre-calculated is pitching blind. Run the numbers first, lead with them.

2
Always leave with a named next action and a date

Every Sean call ends with "I'll send you X by Y" or "I'll have the upgrade link ready by end of week." This removes ambiguity and keeps deals progressing. Compare this to calls that end with "let me know" — those deals stall. Name the action, name the day, confirm it verbally before hanging up.

3
Present pricing as a choice, not a surprise

Sean frames contract terms as options: "1-year at $2,500 or 3-year at $2,300 — most merchants doing your GMV choose the 3-year for the saving." This gives the merchant agency and moves the conversation from "should I buy?" to "which option?" — a fundamentally different and more closeable question.
